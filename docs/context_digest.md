# RewireScope Context Digest

## Core Category-Defining Thesis

Nature is not declining in place. It is changing shape. Ecological risk becomes visible earlier when composition, topology, timing, environmental forcing, and rewiring are analyzed together. Species lists, habitat extent, biodiversity scores, and static network metrics remain useful, but they are incomplete because they can miss the movement, weakening, rerouting, concentration, or silent loss of ecological dependencies.

The next frontier of nature intelligence is dynamic dependency analysis: reading living systems as changing architectures of interaction rather than static inventories of parts.

## Definition of Rewiring Intelligence

Rewiring Intelligence is the capacity to detect, classify, and forecast changes in ecological network topology and interaction strength under environmental and phenological forcing, then translate those changes into functional, operational, and financial meaning.

It is not another biodiversity score and should not collapse every signal into a single universal index. It is a structured, evidence-gated claim system about how ecological dependencies move through time.

## Why Interaction Architecture Is Irreducible

Interaction architecture is the irreducible unit of ecological risk because many relevant failure modes arise through relationships among taxa, not through isolated counts of taxa. Networks reveal what depends on what, which interactions are load-bearing, where redundancy is distributed or concentrated, where cascades can propagate, and which functions depend on indirect support.

Inventories answer what is present. Network structure answers what can hold, what can substitute, what can cascade, and what can fail while the parts still appear present. For interaction-mediated fragility, a relational representation is the minimum analytical representation needed to ask the right questions.

## Why Edge Loss Can Precede Species Loss

Interactions can disappear before the participating species are locally lost. A pollinator can remain in a meadow but stop visiting a dependent plant. A predator can remain in a watershed but no longer regulate a prey population. A frugivore can persist while seed dispersal of a particular plant disappears.

This means species presence can produce a false sense of ecological continuity. The node remains visible to the inventory, while the edge that carried function has already failed. Edge loss is therefore a distinct failure mode, not just a softer version of species loss.

## Why Timing Determines Whether an Edge Executes

Ecological dependencies are not only spatial and topological. They execute through time: flowering windows, pollinator emergence, spawning periods, prey pulses, migration intervals, diel activity windows, moisture thresholds, and thermal envelopes.

Phenological drift is a temporal operator on network edges. If interacting partners drift at different rates, or if both drift relative to a fixed regulatory, financial, or sampling calendar, an interaction that remains possible on a static graph can stop firing in the field. Timing determines whether a nominal edge is an executed ecological event.

## Why Rewiring Is Not Automatically Collapse or Resilience

Networks change. The scientific question is not whether change occurred, but what kind of dependency trajectory the change implies.

Rewiring can preserve function by opening substitute pathways. It can also mask fragility by concentrating function into fewer taxa, seasons, or pathways. It can simplify the system, weaken control links, or erase assumed dependencies without immediate species loss. More movement is not automatically more resilience, and fewer observed changes are not automatically stability.

## Four Rewiring Categories

### Adaptive

Adaptive rewiring occurs when partner shifts or interaction-strength changes preserve function, increase redundancy, or stabilize service delivery under new conditions without creating a material new fragility.

### Compensatory-but-Fragile

Compensatory-but-fragile rewiring occurs when function appears to persist, but the architecture supporting it narrows. Service delivery continues through fewer species, fewer seasons, fewer pathways, or more concentrated generalist dependence. The system is spending redundancy to maintain apparent continuity.

### Degradative

Degradative rewiring occurs when the network reorganizes toward simplification, lower redundancy, weaker control links, reduced specialization, lower modular containment, trait homogenization, or declining functional proxies. Increased rewiring under disturbance can fall into this category when it reflects forced partner switching in an impoverished option set.

### Silent Edge Failure

Silent edge failure occurs when the species remain present and the interaction is still assumed to exist, but the ecological event no longer executes. This can result from phenological mismatch, spatial decoupling, thermal-window shifts, altered activity periods, or missing threshold conditions.

## Evidence Hierarchy

### Observed Rewiring

Observed rewiring requires raw pairwise interaction edges, repeated time bins, identifiable taxa, and direct evidence of edge births, deaths, persistence, partner switching, or interaction-strength shifts.

Allowed claims: direct temporal rewiring visualization, observed edge turnover, observed partner switching, and observed interaction-strength change.

### Partial Historical Contrast

Partial historical contrast applies when data support comparison across historical and contemporary networks or a small number of periods, but not dense temporal animation or event-level rewiring through time.

Allowed claims: historical interaction loss, partial contrast, loss of co-occurrence, phenological overlap change, and bounded interaction-extinction narratives.

### Inferred Dynamic Dependency

Inferred dynamic dependency applies to abundance, occurrence, phenology, trait, diet-prior, environmental, or statistical association data where links are modeled rather than directly observed.

Allowed claims: inferred association, inferred dependency, candidate trophic timing risk, modeled overlap, rolling association networks, and uncertainty-labeled hypotheses.

### Metrics-Only

Metrics-only applies where only derived network statistics, turnover summaries, or published aggregate metrics are available without raw edge-level data.

Allowed claims: validation, comparison with published metrics, interpretive context, and screening-grade signals. Metrics-only sources cannot reconstruct full networks or direct rewiring events.

## Claim Boundary for the POC

Observed interaction networks can support direct rewiring visualization when raw pairwise edges are present across repeated time bins. RMBL / CaraDonna is the primary candidate for this claim, pending raw-column audit.

Partial historical or seasonal datasets can support bounded contrast and classification if raw pairwise edges exist, but they must not be presented as dense continuous rewiring unless the temporal structure actually supports it.

Abundance-only, occurrence-only, phenology-only, environmental, plankton, or fisheries datasets can support inferred dependency analysis only. Any network edges built from those sources must be labeled as inferred and must carry method and uncertainty labels.

Inferred links must never be described as observed rewiring. This boundary is central to the scientific integrity of RewireScope.
